Butter Naan Recipe

Description

Naan is a leavened flatbread, originating from the Indian subcontinent, known for its slightly puffy and soft texture. It's typically cooked in a tandoor, a clay oven, which imparts a characteristic blistered appearance and slightly charred taste

Ingredients

  • Flour
  • Yogurt/Buttermilk
    • Butter
  • Salt
  • Sugar
  • Fresh Herbs

Method

  1. Add 2 cups all-purpose flour, teaspoon baking powder, half teaspoon baking soda, half teaspoon salt and 2 teaspoon sugar to a large mixing bowl

  2. Then pour 2 tablespoon butter and half cup yogurt. Mix all ingredients well knead.

  3. Divide the dough to 6 parts and make round balls tucking the edges inside.

  4. Flour the rolling area very lightly. Too much flour will toughen the naans. Place a dough ball and flatten it.Roll or stretch the dough to a oval or a elongated shape. Sprinkle the toppings and then lightly roll it again. You can make all the naans.

  5. Heat a griddle or tawa. If the pan is not hot enough then the naan will not rise well. So ensure it is hot but not extremely hot. Gently remove the naan from the counter and place it on the hot griddle. Soon you will see bubbles on the naan. You can also cook them covered.
